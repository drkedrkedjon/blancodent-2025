## Did you know...?

In Spain, according to the 2005 National Health Survey, between 85-90% of the population over 35 years old suffers from some form of periodontal disease, mainly gingivitis. The most destructive manifestation is periodontitis or pyorrhoea, affecting 30-38% of adults over 35.

## What else should you know?

- Dental plaque: is the main factor in periodontal or gum diseases. Plaque is a thin and harmful layer or film, which forms continuously as a result of the bacteria living in our mouths (over 700 types), and its accumulation causes gingivitis and periodontitis.

- Gingivitis: is one of the most common dental conditions in humans. It is reversible and can appear at any age. This superficial inflammation of the gum can be healed with a professional dental cleaning. However, if gingivitis persists and there are other factors such as stress, smoking, diabetes, or a possible genetic predisposition, periodontitis will eventually develop. The clearest early sign of gum inflammation is bleeding, whether during brushing or spontaneously. Healthy gums do not bleed.

- Periodontitis or Pyorrhoea: is a deep infection of the gum and the other tissues that support the tooth, leading to bone loss. In addition to bleeding during brushing or spontaneously, you may notice sensitivity to cold, tooth mobility or separation, gum recession, and bad breath.

## What are the consequences of periodontal disease?

If not treated properly, and without regular periodontal maintenance, these dental conditions will worsen until they cause tooth loss. Furthermore, scientific evidence shows that periodontal infection affects general health: it increases cardiovascular risk, complicates blood sugar control, favours lung diseases, and causes complications during pregnancy, increasing the risk of premature birth.

Smokers are up to three times more likely to suffer from pyorrhoea, and as tobacco hinders and reduces blood flow, gum bleeding may not appear, making early detection more difficult. Therefore, smokers should pay special attention to their gum health, as well as try to quit smoking.

## How are they treated?

Controlling dental plaque is the best prevention for periodontal diseases. In addition to regular visits to your dental clinic, each person must take responsibility for their daily oral hygiene by brushing their teeth and interdental spaces at least twice a day. In our Clinic, we will explain how to improve brushing techniques and the use of floss or interdental brushes.

If it is gingivitis, it is treated with professional prophylaxis or dental cleaning; if you suffer from periodontitis, with scaling and root planing, cleaning below the gum. In advanced periodontitis, minor surgeries may be required. Along with regular cleanings, following an appropriate maintenance programme will strengthen your periodontal health and prevent relapses, ensuring long-term control of periodontal disease and avoiding any type of advanced periodontitis.

Removing dental plaque is the best prevention. Professional dental cleaning and maintenance will guarantee us complete dental health.
