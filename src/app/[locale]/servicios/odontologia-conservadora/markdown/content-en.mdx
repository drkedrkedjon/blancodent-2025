## Preserving teeth.

Conservative dentistry is a speciality that includes all the dental treatments needed to preserve the patient's natural teeth. Ultimately, this type of dental treatment seeks to avoid the extraction of damaged teeth.

Thanks to aesthetic materials such as composites, lost dental tissue can be replaced, whether due to decay or fracture. Treating dental decay with fillings is a very common and simple procedure. After removing the affected dental tissue, the cavity is cleaned and filled with aesthetic, visually pleasing materials. The aim is to restore the affected tooth to return proper chewing function.

## Composite veneers.

This is a minimally invasive treatment to improve your smile. Morphological alterations of the teeth, gaps, or dark colour can be corrected. These are very thin composite sheets placed over your teeth to achieve an optimal aesthetic result. The treatment is painless, simple, and does not require tooth preparation, thus preserving their structural strength. Composite dental veneers provide a natural appearance, have a hardness similar to dental enamel, and the same natural look.

## Dental inlays.

In cases of significant loss of dental structure, materials such as ceramics and composite resins are used to restore the tooth. It is a fragment of aesthetic material that mimics the shape of the affected tooth and is cemented onto it. Complete restoration is achieved, and chewing is restored without the need to prepare the tooth or remove healthy tissue.

## Dental crowns and caps

When the tooth is very damaged and does not have enough remaining structure to function, a dental crown is made for its rehabilitation. Teeth can be worn down by decay, bruxism, or dental trauma.

A dental crown or cap is a fixed prosthesis that can be made of porcelain, zirconia, or another aesthetic material, and covers the entire tooth. They are also a good aesthetic option, as they perfectly mimic natural teeth.

One of the main risks for the patient is to lose one of their teeth permanently. To prevent this, a dental crown is one of the best options to help you keep them.
