## What is orthodontics?

Orthodontics is the speciality whose aim is to balance and achieve harmony in the masticatory system, both in children and adults, so it is never too late to start this dental treatment. It involves moving the teeth to improve their function, aesthetics, and bite.

With orthodontics, the ideal position is sought between all the teeth in relation to the jaws and the joint that connects them to the base of the skull. It also ensures proper bite function, taking into account the anatomical differences of each patient.

It is important to note that orthodontics is much more than an aesthetic solution, more than just aligning the teeth. It improves dental health and the healthy life of the teeth, gums, and jaw joint, thus providing overall oral well-being.

The first step is to carry out an orthodontic study to obtain a diagnosis and plan the necessary treatment.

## How do I know if I need orthodontics?

The need for this dental treatment is different for each person. Each case is very individual and personalised; no two cases are the same. The aim is to move the teeth to their ideal position so that they bite together correctly. By balancing the contacts, they usually remain healthy for many years.

## Main reasons for orthodontic treatment:

### To obtain and maintain space:

When baby teeth are replaced, there may not be enough space for the permanent teeth. Orthodontic appliances are used to maintain or create the necessary space for the permanent teeth to grow in the correct position.

### Dental crowding:

To align crooked or crowded teeth, improving the appearance of your smile and preventing problems caused by poor dental hygiene. Properly aligned teeth function better, are easier to clean, and do not wear down unnecessarily. The development of cavities due to crowding caused by poor dental hygiene is avoided. Crowded teeth create perfect spaces for bacteria and dental plaque, which are difficult to remove with daily cleaning and are the main cause of cavities and tooth loss.

### A good bite needs its ideal position:

A poorly positioned bite causes tooth wear and subsequent loosening. Deterioration of the jaw bones and joints develops, with consequences such as migraines, ear noise, neck pain, vertigo, etc.

## The most common bite problems are:

### Crossbite:

One or more upper teeth close behind or outside the lower teeth.

### Open bite:

When there is a gap between the upper and lower front teeth because they do not close together, but the back teeth do.

### Increased overbite:

The upper teeth completely cover the lower teeth when the bite is closed.

### Deviated midline:

The centre between the upper teeth does not coincide with the centre or midline of the lower teeth.

### Gum recession:

Due to poor positioning of the teeth, they receive excessive and harmful force, causing gum recession and, eventually, loosening. The teeth wear down faster and their lifespan is much shorter. Many people lose their teeth due to overload.

### Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) problems:

This is the joint that connects the two jaws to the base of the skull. When our teeth are in a poor position, the bite is incorrect and this causes degenerative processes in the temporomandibular joint. The muscles work harder to try to stabilise the joint, causing muscle pain, neck and ear pain, and also migraines.

## Types of orthodontics

### Interceptive orthodontics, for children:

This type of orthodontics, as the name suggests, intercepts the problem before it occurs. It is carried out with fixed and removable appliances that help maintain the space between the teeth by acting on the supporting bones. The appliances gradually expand the jaws, moving them to the desired position, preventing dental crowding and other future permanent dental problems.

### Corrective orthodontics:

For both young people and adults, it serves to correct functional and aesthetic problems. The choice of orthodontic system is made together with the professional, depending on each case.

## The most commonly used systems are:

### Fixed orthodontics with brackets:

Brackets are placed on the outer surface of the tooth. There is also a more aesthetic type of bracket that is placed on the inner surface of the teeth. Both systems work in the same way: by changing the thickness of the wire, the necessary force is achieved to move the teeth to the desired position.

Brackets placed on the outer surface are one of the most popular techniques due to their simplicity, speed, and good value for money.

Until a few years ago, brackets were considered for children or teenagers. But today, adult orthodontics is a technique that has become widespread in recent times, even though it has traditionally been associated with children or adolescents. The use of brackets in adulthood has become common practice. Their use has become much more normalised, which means that many adults who need this treatment are taking the step and opting for brackets.

### Invisible orthodontics with aligners:

Invisible orthodontics is a dental treatment with transparent plastic splints or aligners, which, as they are changed, alter the position of the teeth, moving them to the desired position. The splints are changed every 15 days for a new set so that the teeth move to their optimal position. They are completely customised, imperceptible, and very comfortable.

Both the number of sets required and the duration of the dental treatment will depend on the complexity of the case.

Many people do not feel comfortable using brackets. That is why dentists in the field of orthodontics look for solutions that make the process of dental alignment easier and more manageable.

And invisible orthodontics (the best-known being Invisalign) is the result of specialists' research. It is an innovative and cutting-edge technique that many patients end up choosing for aesthetic reasons. Invisible orthodontics is designed to improve dental health and is the most discreet orthodontic treatment available.

In addition, it can cover a wide range of orthodontic cases such as: spaced teeth, excessively crowded teeth, or crossbite.

## How long will my orthodontic treatment last?

Each case is different and may vary, but as a general rule, we can estimate that most cases are resolved in one to two years.

The final phase is very important and is carried out with retainers.

Once the orthodontic treatment is finished, retainers are made to prevent the teeth from returning to their previous position. If this phase is underestimated, the teeth may move over time and return to their previous position. To prevent this, upper and lower transparent splints (to be worn at night) are provided, or, if necessary, fixed retainers.

This way, the result obtained with orthodontic treatment will be maintained for life.
