## The effects of tobacco on dental health

The use of tobacco can lead to mouth cancer and/or periodontitis. Sensitivity to hot and cold can also develop. It also worsens dental processes (for example, after a tooth extraction or oral surgery). It is also a cause of bad breath, stains on the teeth/tongue, and tartar build-up on the teeth. Most of these effects are or can be irreversible.

## Differences between types of smokers

There are no differences, as all types contribute to the development of cancer. Perhaps not lung cancer, but they can increase the likelihood of developing throat and lip cancer. If you are a smoker, you should consider quitting this habit, because although we are concerned here with oral health, it is a matter of general health. Tobacco can kill, and this has been clinically proven.
