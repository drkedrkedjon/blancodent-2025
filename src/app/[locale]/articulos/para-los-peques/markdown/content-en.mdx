## At what age should children go to the dentist?

It is recommended to visit the dentist 6 months after the first tooth appears and before the first year. By attending at an early age, the dentist can detect and act on any possible anomaly, thus helping the development of healthy teeth.

## About cleaning the mouth before the first tooth:

It is necessary to clean the mouth from the first days after birth. It is recommended to use small cloths to remove plaque after each feeding.

## Cavities in children

From the eruption of the first tooth, cavities can be detected. This happens when the baby is fed with a bottle containing sugary water or fruit juice at night. In this case, the teeth are under acid attack and this, in the long term, can cause problems. It is recommended to give the baby a bottle with water and not to offer a dummy with honey or sugar.

## Is teething painful?

Yes, because during this process the gums are inflamed and sore. The pain can be reduced by massaging the inflamed area with a finger or a damp cloth. The natural process of teething does not usually cause a fever. If your baby has a very high fever, you should see a paediatrician, as it would then be a different problem.

## What about thumb-sucking?

This habit should disappear between the ages of two and four. Sometimes the habit can continue, which can lead to other problems, both physical and psychological (in their relationship with others).
