## What are dental prostheses?

It is very important for our dental health to replace lost teeth immediately. Dental prostheses are the artificial, partial or total restoration of dental anatomy. With the exception of the four wisdom teeth, any loss of teeth has a negative impact on the rest of the teeth. And it will not only affect our mouth, but can also involve our ears, neck, jaw, and cause difficulty when chewing and digesting food... That is why dental prostheses are so important when it comes to restoring dental anatomy.

From an aesthetic point of view, the lack of several teeth gives the face a less youthful appearance, as deeper lines appear around the mouth and nose.

Dental prostheses are treatments that allow the replacement of lost teeth and molars through two systems:

## Removable dental prosthesis

These dental prostheses are a set of teeth that can be removed and put in at will. To keep them in place, they have retainers such as hooks, telescopic crowns, or a system of attachments or invisible clasps.

## Fixed dental prosthesis

This type of dental prosthesis allows for better adaptation due to its shape and is more similar to natural teeth. It cannot be removed at will, which is why it has a very positive psychological effect. The adaptation period is almost non-existent. As the prosthesis has less volume, it feels more comfortable. Both systems can also be combined, which is called a MIXED dental treatment.

Both systems are equally effective and thanks to them, not only aesthetics are restored, but also speech and the chewing function of the mouth.
