## About anaesthetics

Dental anaesthesia is a recommended and widely used technique in dental clinics to prevent pain or discomfort during medical treatment. In some cases, anxiety and fear can prevent many people from attending their treatment. In these cases, sedatives can be used. Current advances in treatments and medicines can help reduce or eliminate pain and anxiety.

Depending on each patient's personal characteristics and medical history, such as the medication they are taking or their general state of health, the dentist will choose one technique or another, adapting it to each patient.

Our aim at Blancodent Dental Clinic is to offer you not only the excellence of our services but also to ensure that your visit and the treatment you receive are as pleasant and comfortable as possible.

## Analgesics

Analgesics such as paracetamol, ibuprofen, and aspirin can reduce pain before or after treatment.

## Local anaesthesia

- Topical, applied superficially to the gums and used to prevent the pain of the injection.
- Injectable, used to prevent pain in specific areas during treatment. It is recognised by the "numb lips" effect.

## Sedatives

These medicines are used in the process of preparing the patient in cases of anxiety and can be used together with local anaesthesia.
