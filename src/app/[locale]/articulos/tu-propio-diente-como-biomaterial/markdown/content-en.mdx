## Take advantage of your own extracted tooth as a source of biomaterial to improve the healing of your implant

It is especially indicated for cases where there is not enough bone in the patient's jaw, which is associated with a high risk of implant loss. This innovative technological process, used at Blancodent, allows us to use dental biomaterial extracted from the patient themselves, thus avoiding the use of a synthetic or animal-derived product. With this process, the rejection rate is practically zero as it is a material made with the patient's own DNA.

This involves a novel device called the "Smart Dentin Grinder," which allows us to use our own tooth after extraction. It is prepared for immediate use at the time of implant placement or can be stored for future use.

### How does it work?

Thanks to the patented SMART GRINDER process, developed by Prof. Binderman, it is possible to transform the extracted tooth into a bone graft.

Prof. Binderman's group has standardised this procedure and developed the necessary device. We are one of the pioneering clinics to bring it to the Canary Islands.

The process is as follows: first, the extracted teeth are cleaned, disinfected, and ground in the device. The resulting structure is granular in appearance and can be shaped as needed, adapting to the area where there is not enough bone to place the implants.

Scientific explanation: The composition of the tooth is almost identical to that of human bone, in terms of calcium and phosphorus ions; 70% are mineral substances, 20% organic components (especially collagen), and the remaining 10% is water, which makes it ideal for use as an autograft.

This autologous graft does not provoke an immune reaction; its incorporation is quick and effective, and in a few months it becomes bone.
